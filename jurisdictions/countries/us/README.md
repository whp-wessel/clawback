# United States (US)

## Context

The US has extensive federal open-data infrastructure for procurement and spending:

- **USASpending.gov** — Federal spending data (contracts, grants, loans, direct payments).
- **FPDS** (Federal Procurement Data System) — Detailed contract-level procurement data.
- **SAM.gov** — System for Award Management; entity registration, exclusions, contract opportunities.
- **FOIA.gov** — Federal Freedom of Information Act request portal.

## Relevant legislation

- Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)
- Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)
- DATA Act (Digital Accountability and Transparency Act)

## Focus areas

- Contract splitting below simplified acquisition thresholds ($250K)
- Sole-source justification patterns
- Grant-to-related-entity networks
- Debarment and exclusion list cross-referencing
